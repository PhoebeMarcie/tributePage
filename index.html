About Nina

  • Feb 21 1933 Born Eunice Kathleen Waymon to Mary Kate Waymon and John Divine Waymon in Tyson North Carolina.

  • 1938-1950 She became the official pianist for Methodist church of Tyron(Her mother's church) and began piano lessons with Mrs Muriel Mazzonavich after forming The Waymon Sisters Group with her 2 sisters.

    She had her first activist moment at age 12 when she refused to perform during a recital until her parents got the seats that they had been forced to vacate for white people. At age 17 she graduated Valedictorian of her highschool class and later went to Julliard school of music. After auditioning at the Curtis Institute of Music, she was rejected due to what she claimed was racism.

  • 1952-1970 In 1952 she worked as a pianist for Anne Smith wile taking lessons with Vladimir Solokoff. She later took a job as a singer-pianist in Atlantic City and took on the stage name Nina Simone and later signed with Bethlehem Records. Around the 1960's she actively got involed in activism by writting and performing songs that became activism anthems, like "Missipi Godamn","Four women", "Young Gifted And Black" and "Why(The King is Dead)". She got involved in rallies lie the Selma to Mongomery marches and even refused to pay her taxes as her way of protesting against American involvement in the Vietnam war

  • 1971-1980 Divorced her husband and manager Andy Stroud and later move to Barbados. Her brother, Sam Waymon became her new manager. She later became involved with the Barbadoss PrimeMinister Earl Barrow. Some time later she moved to Liberia as an expatriate. After moving back to America, she got arrested but was immediately released for withholding taxes. It was also during this period that she was awarded her first honorary doctorate degree and also got diagnosed with bipolar disorder.

  • 1981-2003 She got awarded ownership of 52 of her master recordings. This time was marked with her constant trouble wit the law. She got two suspended sentences for violence. In 2000 she became a Grammy Hall of Fame Inductee. In the year 2001, her mother died. She held her last concert on June 29 2002 and was later diagnosed with breast cancer that year. On April 19 2003, the Curtis Institute of Music named her honorary Doctor in Music and Humanities.

    On April 21 2003, Nina died from breast cancer complications in her home in Carry-le-Rouet France.
